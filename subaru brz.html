Subaru BRZ


      The Subaru BRZ is a compact, rear-wheel-drive sports car that was first introduced in 2012 as a collaboration between Subaru and Toyota. It shares its platform with the Toyota 86 (formerly the Scion FR-S in the U.S.), offering a budget-friendly, performance-focused driving experience with an emphasis on agility, handling, and driving pleasure.

      Engine:

    • The BRZ is powered by a naturally aspirated 2.4-liter four-cylinder engine (as of the 2022 model year), delivering around 228 horsepower and 184 lb-ft of torque. It offers a choice of a 6-speed manual or 6-speed automatic transmission.

      • Performance:

    • The car is known for its light weight (around 2,800 lbs), balanced handling, and responsive steering, making it an ideal car for driving enthusiasts who enjoy cornering and spirited driving. It has a low center of gravity due to its boxer engine layout.

      • Design:

    • The BRZ features a sleek, aerodynamic design with a sharp front end and aggressive stance. Its cabin focuses on a driver-centric layout with simple, functional controls.

      • Technology:

    • The latest models include modern infotainment features like Apple CarPlay, Android Auto, and a touchscreen display, along with safety features like adaptive cruise control and lane-keep assist.
